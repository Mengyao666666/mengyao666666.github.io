Hi! I'm a math enthusiast, also a beginner shaman ;)

{{< figure src="/images/path.jpg" alt="Cusco landscape near the Temple of the Moon on Saqsaywaman hill" caption="Cusco — near the Temple of the Moon, on Saqsaywaman hill." class="about-photo" >}}

As an undergraduate in China, I studied Computer Science and Finance at HUST — a major whose name was already slightly suspicious, and whose curriculum turned out to be exactly as literal as it sounded: a rather mechanical stitching-together of computer science courses and finance courses. My early practical experience was mostly in the primary market, where I looked at many high-tech companies. Somewhere along the way, after seeing enough emerging technologies, I realized that I was not only interested in finance. I was deeply fascinated by technology itself.

That curiosity brought me to UIUC, where I studied Information Science and explored compilers, Linux systems, and lower-level computing. I enjoyed getting closer to the foundations of systems, but I eventually found myself returning to the same question: how do we model complex things?



So I started teaching myself mathematics, including real analysis, Fourier analysis, and projection theory. Math began as something I studied for fun — a quiet hobby I returned to when I wanted to relax. Unexpectedly, it also became a companion to my shamanic practice. Both are, at their core, practices of attention.



Welcome to my world. I hope you find something interesting here, or maybe just a strange way of looking at things.
